Who is James Webb?

James Edwin Webb (October 7, 1906 – March 27, 1992) was an American government official who served as Undersecretary of State from 1949–1952. He was also the second appointed administrator of NASA from February 14, 1961, to October 7, 1968.

NASA is set to launch the James Webb Space Telescope — the world's most powerful space observatory. Webb's first year will include bold searches for primordial galaxies and habitable planets. Webb could spot gold being forged in violent cosmic collisions and lava raining on distant planets.

Webb's infrared vision makes it a powerful time machine that will peer back over 13.5 billion years, to a time shortly after the Big Bang. Webb's infrared sensitivity will not only look back farther in time but will also reveal dramatically more information about stars and galaxies in the early Universe.

The telescope will be placed in orbit about a million miles from Earth, roughly four times the distance of our planet from the Moon. Unlike Hubble, the current premier space telescope that revolves around the planet, Webb will orbit the Sun.

The telescope is designed to last at least five and a half years (six months calibration plus five years science operations), but with a goal of ten years. The limiting factor is expected to be fuel to maintain its halo orbit, of which there is enough for at least ten years.
